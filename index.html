Canada First Investing

A macroeconomic approach to capital growth through resilient domestic infrastructure and foundational corporate sectors.

In an era defined by global supply chain reconfigurations and shifting cross-border dynamics, focusing heavily on domestic economic foundations offers unmatched structural advantages, legal stability, and intrinsic currency risk mitigation.

National Resource Infrastructure

Canada boasts some of the most critical structural assets in the modern world. This mandate targets established entities that own and operate key midstream energy assets, transportation channels, and processing frameworks. These organizations typically possess deep economic moats that insulate them effectively from speculative, hyper-volatile technology cycles.

Cross-Provincial Corporate Tailwinds

Our quantitative selection framework identifies firms structurally poised to outpace standard metrics due to favorable regulatory changes or regional demographic shifts. Key considerations include:

  • Provincial Fiscal Trajectories: Identifying areas with robust corporate tax revenues and stable public sector infrastructure project commitments.
  • Banking Sector Stability: Leveraging the historical and systemic resilience of Canada’s highly regulated core financial system.
  • Consumer Defensiveness: Emphasizing utilities, grocery, and communication systems that yield strong cash flows across all economic environments.

Currency Risk Mitigation

For Canadian households, matching long-term liabilities (retirement, education, healthcare) with CAD-denominated assets protects purchasing power from severe global foreign exchange swings. By removing excessive foreign currency volatility, portfolios can focus on organic corporate growth and steady dividend compounding.
